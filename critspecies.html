Critically Endangered Species

Indiana Bat

Background Information

The indiana bat is a small, insectivorous, migratory bat. It hibernates in caves and mines in the winter. Up to 500 bats will hibernate per square foot in the caves. They require forest to forage and roosting.

Endangered Status

The species was originally listed as in danger of extinction in 1966. It is currently still listed.

Eastern Massasauga

Background Information

The eastern massasauga rattlesnake is a small pit viper that occurs in the northern midwest United States and Ontario, Canada.

Endangered Status

The species were listed as threatened under the Endangered Species Act in 2016. This was due primarily to habitat loss.

Mitchell's Satyr

Background Information

Mitchell's Satyr is a small, brown butterfly that lives in prairie fen habitats.

Endangered Status

The decline of the species is caused by changes in habitat and hydrology. The suppression of natural disturbance events that are important to the fen habitat have also been detrimental.

Poweshiek Skipperling

Background Information

The poweshiek skipperling is one of seven butterfly species in the genus Oarisma. They are small butterflies that were once found in native prairie in the United States. It has been killed out in a lot of the states. The only states it is left in is Michigan, manitoba, and maybe in Wisconsin.

Endangered Status

It was listed as federally endangered in 2014 and critical habitat was designated in 2015. There a conservation movements to protect.

American Burying Beetle

Background Information

The american burying beetle is the largest carrion beetle in North America. This species reaches 1 to 1.8 inches in length.

Endangered Status

Risks such as habitat loss or alteration and artificial lights affect most populations. All remaining populations have some risks associated with areas of urban or suburban development.

Snuffbox

Background Information

The snuffbox is a small to medium sized mussel.

Endangered Status

The snuffbox is currently listed as endangered. It currently resides in a bunch of different states.

Smallmouth Salamandar

Background Information

The small-mouth salamander is a species of mole salamandar found currently in central United States. It lives in moist pine woodlands and deciduous forest bottomlands, tallgrass prairies and more.

Endangered Status

It is listed as endangered. It is is only known in Michigan and on Pelee Island in Lake Erie.

Kirtland's Snake

Background Information

The Kirtland's snake is a nonvenomous snake. It is a small and slender snake. Adults reach a total of 12-18 inches. It's habitats are forest, grassland, and wetlands, such as swamps and marshes.

Endangered Status

It is listed as endangered in Michigan.

Copper-bellied Water Snake

Background Information

The Copper-bellied water snake is found in southern Michigan, northern Indiana, and Ohio. It feeds primarily on amphigbians.

Endangered Status

They are currently listed as endangered in Michigan.
